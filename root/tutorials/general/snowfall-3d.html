EduGraf Tutorial: Simulate 3D Snowfall using Koch Snowflakes

In this tutorial, you'll learn how to create a 3D snowfall simulation featuring Koch snowflakes. We'll show you how to draw intricate recursive shapes using triangles and add realistic physical behavior to bring your snowfall to life! This project builds on the foundational classes and concepts already available in EduGraf.

What are Koch Snowflakes?

Koch Snowflakes are self-similar or fractal curves, first described in 1904 by the Swedish mathematician Helge von Koch. The concept begins with a straight line, which is divided into three equal parts. The middle segment is replaced with an equilateral triangle, using the segment as its base. By removing the base of the triangle, the first iteration of the Koch Curve is complete.
